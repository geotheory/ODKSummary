OpenDataKit

ODK is a mobile data collection system made up of two main components. Firstly a customisable Android app for data collection in the field (ODK Collect) and secondly a server for collating records into a single database (ODK Aggregate). ODK Collect will run on your phone/tablet, the data you collect will be sent to the mobile ODK Aggregate server, and you'll then be able to download and visualise it in the field with these pages (ODK Summary).

Setting up ODK Collect

  • Download the ODK Collect app from the Google Play repository. For this you'll need an Internet connection.

  • Once downloaded set up the network connection. Make sure that the device is switched on (as shown in the hardware setup page). On your device, find the network named "MSF_Data1/2", and add the username and password.

  • Next we need to configure ODK Collect to speak to ODK Aggregate: open ODK Collect, go to General Settings/Configure platform settings, then enter the URL http://data.msf:8080/ODKAggregate and the designated username and password.

  • Back in General settings tick the option to Auto send with Wi-Fi.

  • Now get the forms. In the main menu of ODK, go to Get Blank Form, select forms MOAS OPD v.9.5 and MOAS registration form v9.5 and download them.

Collecting data

You're ready to go. To input data select Fill Blank Form and complete it. Swipe from right to left to move through the form or finish it. Mark it as finalized and that's it - the record will be sent to the server. If you need to de-select an option, long-press it till you get the option 'Remove response'.
